Biography

Welcome! I am a PhD student at the Operations Research Center advised by Alexandre Jacquillat and Vikrant Vaze. I am a 2018 recipient of the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. As an undergraduate, I conducted research with the Cybersecurity Research Group at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, DIMACS at Rutgers University, the Mathematics Department at Harvey Mudd College, the Computer Science Department at Pomona College, and the Mathematics Department at Texas A&M University.

My graduate work incorporates optimization, game theory, and analytics toward the integrated design of urban transportation systems. My current projects consider workforce scheduling for on-demand paratransit systems and pricing alliance design for first-mile systems. On the former project I am collaborating with Via Transportation, and on the latter with Özlem Ergun and Chancellor Cynthia Barnhart.

I am passionate about diversity and access in higher education and am currently working to cultivate an inclusive pedagogy. Off campus, you can find me playing classical piano, cooking vegetarian food, marathon-training along the Charles River, and learning how to throw pottery.
